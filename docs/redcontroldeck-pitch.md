# Redcontroldeck — What You Get That the Archive Can't Give You

The Internet Archive is extraordinary — thousands of live recordings from the Chicago music scene, freely available, meticulously preserved. But as a listening experience, it's a filing cabinet. You get a list of files, some metadata, and a basic player.

Redcontroldeck is built on top of that catalog and turns it into something you actually want to spend time in.

## Context that puts you in the room

Every show in the collection surfaces the weather from that exact night — temperature, conditions, sunset time. Where available, it pulls in what was happening in Chicago sports on the same date. The idea is simple: if you see a February show where the high was 26°F and the sun set at 5:36 PM, you can immediately put yourself there. That's not trivia — it's immersion.

## Time Travel: reconstructing a night out

One of the app's signature features finds something hidden in the data: Jacobs frequently recorded multiple bands on the same bill, the same night, at the same venue. Redcontroldeck surfaces those and assembles them into a single tile. Tap it and the whole evening loads into your queue in order — opener, middle act, headliner. You're not just listening to a recording. You're going to the show.

## A discovery layer the Archive doesn't have

The Discover tab surfaces things you'd never find by browsing a file list: shows recorded on today's date across all years, the most-played recordings in the collection, and those reconstructed full-evening bills. It's designed to give you a prompt every time you open it — something to follow, something to explore.

## The long view

This collection spans decades. Redcontroldeck is designed to help you feel that arc — to notice that a band you're hearing in a small club in 1994 is about to release something important, or that two artists who'll later collaborate are sharing a bill tonight. That kind of context is what turns a good recording into a genuinely compelling listen.

## The short version

> The Archive preserves the recordings. Redcontroldeck makes them worth exploring — with the context, the curation, and the features to turn a catalog into an experience.
